The president of Simba, Evans Aveva has denied the report of signing up defender Mbuyu Twite of the left Yanga.

However, speaking to this newspaper, the president of Simba Club Evans Aveva has denied those reports and said that the defender from the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) has no chance in the team's squad.
"Twite has no chance in the current squad of Simba because we have many players who are small and with great talents in his position," Aveva has said.
He said that he is heard that Mbuyu Twite was left out by Yanga and that Simba needs and said that rumors has no any truth.
He said that the report of their coach Joseph Omog, shows that the club needs a goalkeeper and already they have appointed the goalkeeper Daniel Agenda, from Madega.
Apart from signing that goalkeeper, Simba also have signed an attacking mid-fielder, James DeBarge, but he added that he also need one striker that will work with Laudit Mavugo and Ibrahim Ajib.
Mbuyu has been reported by Yanga after the club signing a mid-fielder from Zesco United Club of Zambia and completed the number seven players of foreign players.
Four years ago Mbuyu has signed a previous contract with Simba, but later he got into that club to register Yanga which he helped to win various clubs in the Tanzania Mainland Premier League (CAF).
