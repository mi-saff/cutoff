
The University of Lands will start a research on the port of Tanga.
The Huria University has completed the construction and laying of equipment in the port of Tanga in one of the research projects that are being done in that college for the purpose of testing the real situation in the sea.
The Deputy Head of that college, Professor Adolf Mshanga said that when he was reading a speech during the 10 university graduation ceremony that took place at the college in Dar es Salaam.
Professor Mshenga said that together with the responsibility of giving graduates at different professions for development of the country, the institution has a responsibilities of doing research and to provide professional advice, thus through the Government of Norway in collaboration with the government of Tanzania has been able to make that construction.
"The purpose of this project is to help improve communication between the port and its users as the right to be provided about the real state of the reserve will help make the correct decisions of using the port also, those reports will be great help to other stakeholders like fishermen, tourists together with various researchers," said Professor Mshenga.
He added that in giving advice and professional services, the institution has implemented various government projects, public and private institutions in all sectors that have been taught in the university and to give opportunities to employees and give opportunities to employees to get experience in various professions they studied.
In addition, Professor Mshenga has thanked the fifth term government for continuing to give the funds and other donations to run for college activities where for the financial year 2016 / 2017 the government has set aside a total of 5 billion shillings for the budget of the university.
While speaking about the graduates, Professor Mshenga has clarified that a total of 968 students will be awarded degrees and diplomas, whereby 868 students will be awarded certificates and doctorate degree to 6 students.
On his part, the deputy minister of education, science and technology, Engineer Stella Manyanya said that there is still a big challenge for girls to join science subjects because the number has shown that female graduates are estimated to have 33 percent of all the students thus he has appealed to girls to use opportunities to study those studies without fear.
Engineer Manyanya has advised youths to focus in education that will help them to be self-employed more than what they need to be employed since that way it will help to improve economically quickly and contribute to the development of the nation.
